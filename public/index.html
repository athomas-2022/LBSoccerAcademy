The Standard every coach carries.

Five non-negotiables, posted where every coach can see them — the same at every age, from a kindergartner's first Saturday to a Next XI session. It's why the sessions work.

  • Touches

    Max touches, always. No lines, no laps, no long lectures. If a kid is standing still, the drill is wrong.

  • Brave

    Praise the brave, not just the skilled. We reward the kid who tries the move and loses it — we build players who want the ball.

  • Play

    Every session ends with a game. Games are why kids come back. We always finish playing.

  • Youngest

    Our best, most energetic coaches go with our youngest kids. That's where love of the game is built.

  • System

    One vocabulary at every age, so an eight-year-old and a varsity starter speak the same soccer.

  • And above all — every kid known by name. Being known is the number-one reason a kid comes back.

Questions,
answered.

The things families ask most. Still wondering something? Just ask us.

How much does it cost?

It's free — the year-round program costs families nothing to play, ever. No child is turned away for cost, and if gear is the barrier, our gear closet covers cleats, shin guards, and balls, no questions asked.

What ages and grades can play?

Kindergarten through 8th grade, boys and girls, in three age tiers — Grassroot Eagles (K–2), Eagles Academy (3–5), and Next XI Eagles (6–8) — so a child can grow through the whole pathway.

My child has never played before. Is that okay?

Absolutely — that's exactly who we're built for. Every tier meets kids where they are, starting with the basics in a fun, low-pressure setting. No experience needed, ever.

What if we can't afford cleats or a ball?

No child is turned away for gear. Our gear closet — stocked by community donations — provides cleats, shin guards, and balls free of charge. Just ask an organizer. It's confidential and judgment-free.

Is it for boys or girls?

Both — equally. It's one program and one standard for the boys and girls sides alike. Kids are grouped by age, not gender, and everything we run serves both.

Will this conflict with rec league or other sports?

No — the Academy is built to be in addition, never instead. We run in the gaps (off-nights, weekend mornings, and the off-season) and coordinate the calendar so kids can play multiple sports without burning out or being forced to choose. Many kids do both.

We already play travel or club. Is this instead of that?

Not at all — keep your club for games and competition. The Academy layers free technique work and the Eagles system on top, in the slots your league doesn't use, so your kid keeps developing without ever having to pick one over the other.
