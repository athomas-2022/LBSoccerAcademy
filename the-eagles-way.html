The Standard

The coaching non-negotiables
every affiliated coach carries

Six things hold true at every age, every session, no exceptions. If you only remember six, remember these.

Touches

Max touches, always — no lines, no laps, no long lectures. If a kid is standing still, the drill is wrong.

Brave

Praise the brave, not just the skilled. Reward the kid who tries the move and loses it — that's how they learn it.

Play

Every session ends with a game. Games are why kids come back — never skip the scrimmage to "get through" the plan.

Youngest

Our best, most energetic coaching goes to our youngest kids. Their first taste of soccer has to be joy.

System

One vocabulary at every age, so an 8-year-old and a varsity starter speak the same soccer.

Known

Every kid known by name. It's the number-one reason a kid comes back — greet each one as they arrive.

The Eagles Session Shape

One repeatable template
at any age

Same four beats every time. Kids know the rhythm, you never scramble for a plan, and the ball is out the whole session. Rough splits shown — flex them, but keep the order.

15%
Beat 1 · Arrival

Ball-mastery warmup

Everyone on a ball the moment they arrive — no waiting, no standing. Simple moves, toe-taps, foundations while you learn who's here.

20%
Beat 2 · Teach

Skill of the day

Short teach → reps → a mini-challenge. Demo in under a minute, then let them try it. One idea per session, not five.

40%
Beat 3 · Compete

Small-sided games

The bulk of the session. 1v1, 2v2, 3v3 — the skill of the day shows up inside a real game with a scoreboard.

25%
Beat 4 · Finish

Free scrimmage

End on a game they own. Light touch from you — let them play, celebrate the brave stuff, send them home wanting more.

More ball, less talk. When in doubt, roll another ball out and coach inside the game.

The Curriculum, By Age Band

What to teach, and what
good looks like

Three stages, one connected path. Each stage builds on the last using the same words and the same games, just bigger. Coach the stage in front of you — don't rush a K–2 group into systems, and don't baby a 7th-grade group.

Grassroot EaglesK–2Fun, coordination, ball love — fun is the curriculum
What good looks like

Every kid has their own ball and a smile. Lots of laughing, lots of goals, nobody in a line. They leave asking when they get to come back.

Season themes

  • Dribbling & stopping — run with the ball, freeze on command
  • Changing direction — turns, cuts, dodging "sharks"
  • Shooting & scoring — big goals, everyone scores, celebrate every one
  • Simple 1v1 — me and my ball vs. one friend
Sample session focus

"Traffic & turns" — dribble through a maze of cones, freeze on the whistle, change direction, then a swarm of tiny-goal 1v1s to finish.

Coaching cues (say these)

Little touches, keep it close!
Freeze! Now go the other way!
Wow, great try — go again!
Eagles Academy3–5Real skill — the toolbox that beats a defender
What good looks like

Kids take players on without being told to. First touch settles the ball, heads come up to look, and they've got a go-to move they trust under pressure.

Season themes

  • Dribbling to beat a defender — 1v1 moves: scissor, chop, stepover
  • Passing & receiving surfaces — inside, outside, sole; both feet
  • First touch away from pressure — touch into space, not into feet
  • Small-sided decision-making — pass, dribble, or shoot?
Sample session focus

"Take them on" — warm up a chosen 1v1 move, rep it against a passive then live defender, then 2v2 to goals where beating your player earns a bonus point.

Coaching cues (say these)

Head up — what do you see?
First touch into space.
Try your move — I love the brave.
Next XI Eagles6–8The Eagles system — how we play as a team
What good looks like

Kids know their position and the jobs that come with it. They play out of the back on purpose, win the ball back as a group, and use our words without prompting.

Season themes

  • Team shape & positions — spacing, roles, staying connected
  • Playing out of the back — build calmly, don't just boot it
  • Pressing & transition — win it back fast, react the instant it turns over
  • Set pieces & our vocabulary — corners, throws, restarts; one shared language
Sample session focus

"Out of the back" — position a rondo to keep the ball, build a 4v4+2 that starts from the keeper, then a full small-sided game rewarding a clean build through the thirds.

Coaching cues (say these)

Open up — check your shoulder first.
We lost it — press together, now!
Play through the lines, be patient.
