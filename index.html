Our mission

The LB Soccer Academy exists to give every kid in our district better soccer — not by adding to their week, but by training the coaches they already have in one Eagles way of playing, free of cost, from a first practice all the way to future varsity Eagles.

The Eagles Way

One way of playing,
taught to every coach.

The Eagles Way is the curriculum every affiliated coach is trained in — the Standard, the shape of a session, and what to teach at each age. It's why a kid's soccer gets better without one new thing on your calendar.

The Standard Six non-negotiables every coach carries

  • Touches

    Max touches, always. No lines, no laps, no long lectures. If a kid is standing still, the drill is wrong.

  • Brave

    Praise the brave, not just the skilled. We reward the kid who tries the move and loses it — we build players who want the ball.

  • Play

    Every session ends with a game. Games are why kids come back. We always finish playing.

  • Everyone

    Everyone plays — real minutes for every kid, every session. We never bench a kid to chase a result; you can't develop on the sideline.

  • System

    One vocabulary at every age, so an eight-year-old and a varsity starter speak the same soccer.

  • And above all — every kid known by name. Being known is the number-one reason a kid comes back.

The session shape Same four beats, any age

  1. 15% Beat 1 · Arrival

    Ball-mastery warmup

    Everyone on a ball the moment they arrive — no waiting, no standing. Simple moves and foundations while you see who's here.

  2. 20% Beat 2 · Teach

    Skill of the day

    Short teach → reps → a mini-challenge. Demo in under a minute, then let them try it. One idea per session, not five.

  3. 40% Beat 3 · Compete

    Small-sided games

    The bulk of it. 1v1, 2v2, 3v3 — the day's skill shows up inside a real game with a score.

  4. 25% Beat 4 · Finish

    Free scrimmage

    End on a game they own. Light touch — let them play, celebrate the brave, send them home wanting more.

More ball, less talk. When in doubt, roll another ball out and coach inside the game.

The curriculum What to teach, by age band

Grassroot EaglesK–2 · U6–U8

Fun, coordination, ball love — fun is the curriculum.

What good looks like: every kid has a ball and a smile, nobody stands in a line, and they leave asking when they get to come back.

  • Dribbling & stopping — run, freeze on command
  • Changing direction — turns, cuts, dodge the "sharks"
  • Shooting & scoring — big goals, everyone scores
  • Simple 1v1 — me and my ball vs. one friend
Say these “Little touches, keep it close!” “Freeze — now go the other way!” “Great try — go again!”
Rising Eagles3–5 · U9–U11

Real skill — the toolbox that beats a defender.

What good looks like: kids take players on without being told, first touch settles the ball, and they've got a go-to move they trust under pressure.

  • 1v1 moves — scissor, chop, stepover
  • Passing & receiving — inside, outside, sole; both feet
  • First touch into space — not into feet
  • Small-sided decisions — pass, dribble, or shoot?
Say these “Head up — what do you see?” “First touch into space.” “Try your move — I love the brave.”
Next XI Eagles6–8 · U12–U15

The Eagles system — how we play as a team.

What good looks like: kids know their position and its jobs, play out of the back on purpose, and use our words without prompting.

  • Team shape & positions — spacing, roles, staying connected
  • Playing out of the back — build, don't just boot it
  • Pressing & transition — win it back fast
  • Set pieces & vocabulary — one shared language
Say these “Open up — check your shoulder.” “We lost it — press together!” “Play through the lines, be patient.”

Questions,
answered.

The things coaches and families ask most. Still wondering something? Just ask us.

What is the academy, exactly?

The academy is the coaching, not another team. Eagles staff train the district's rec, travel, and school coaches in one shared way of playing — the Eagles Way — so every kid gets better soccer at the practice they already attend. We don't add sessions to your kid's week; we make the ones they already have great.

How much does it cost?

It's free — families pay nothing, ever. We invest in coaches instead of charging fees, funded by local sponsors. If gear is a barrier, our gear closet covers cleats, shin guards, and balls, no questions asked.

My kid already plays rec or travel — how does this reach them?

Through their coach. When your kid's coach becomes Eagles-Affiliated, their normal practice runs on the Eagles Way — better sessions, more touches, one clear system. Nothing changes on your calendar. Tell us your kid's team on the form and we'll invite their coach.

Do I have to bring my kid to academy sessions?

No. The academy runs through your kid's own team, so there are no extra required sessions. The only academy-run dates are optional Play-with-the-Eagles showcase days — a couple of fun, all-comers events a year — plus game-night appearances.

I'm a coach — what do I get, and what's the commitment?

You get free preseason clinics, a grab-and-go session-plan library for your age group, and a high school coach who mentors your practices all season. The commitment is light: one clinic per season and running your own team the Eagles Way. You keep your team, your roster, and your voice on the field.

What ages and grades does it cover?

Kindergarten through 8th grade, boys and girls, in three curriculum stages — Grassroot Eagles (K–2, U6–U8), Rising Eagles (3–5, U9–U11), and Next XI Eagles (6–8, U12–U15) — that feed straight into varsity. Coaches are trained for the stage they coach.

Is it for boys or girls?

Both — equally. It's one way of playing and one standard for the boys and girls sides alike. Kids are grouped by age, not gender, and everything we teach serves both.

What if a kid can't afford cleats or a ball?

No kid is turned away for gear. Our gear closet — stocked by community donations — provides cleats, shin guards, and balls free of charge. Any affiliated coach can pull from it, or just ask an organizer. It's confidential and judgment-free.
