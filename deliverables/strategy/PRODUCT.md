# Product

## Register

brand

> Two surfaces ship under one identity. The **public "Every Kid Plays" site** is the
> flagship and sets the default register (**brand** — design *is* the product; a family's
> or sponsor's first impression is the thing being made). The **coach's dashboard** is a
> second surface in the **product** register (design *serves* the workflow). When a task
> targets the dashboard, treat it as `product`; everything else defaults to `brand`.

## Users

**The LB Soccer Academy** is the free youth soccer pipeline of the Liberty-Benton Eagles
(Division IV, Findlay OH). It serves the **boys and girls programs equally — one system,
same standard.** Three audiences touch the surfaces:

- **Families** (parents of K–8 kids, some priced out of club soccer) — often arriving on a
  phone, from a flyer or a text, deciding in seconds whether this is "for us." Their job:
  understand it's genuinely free and welcoming, then sign a kid up without friction or shame.
- **Sponsors** (local business owners) — deciding whether to fund it. Their job: see the
  problem, the tiers ($100–$1,000+), the concrete outcome per dollar, and say yes.
- **The coach / co-organizer** (program operator) — running the pipeline. Their job (the
  dashboard): map every athletic kid K–8 by grad year, watch the Four Numbers, work the
  360-day / 12-phase launch plan, track sponsors, and never lose a kid they can't replace.

## Product Purpose

A fixed-talent district builds its future from within — the only way to win a D-IV dynasty is
to **grow our own**: get more local kids playing soccer younger and better, and keep every one
through to varsity. The Academy copies what the best soccer nations do — develop for free, put the
best coaches with the youngest kids, protect free play, build one clear pathway — and applies
it at the district level.

- **Public site** exists to convert a glance into a signed-up kid or a committed sponsor. It
  carries the "Every Kid Plays" promise (free to play, free gear closet, nobody turned
  away) and makes being an Eagle the thing to be in town.
- **Dashboard** exists to run the machine: capture & retention discipline, the Four Numbers
  (capture rate · retention rate · active players · sponsor dollars), the phase plan, sponsors.

Success: more kids playing younger → more touches → better, happier players → packed game
nights → soccer becomes cool → even more kids. The flywheel spins faster every year.

## Brand Personality

**Varsity-proud · welcoming · mission-driven.** The voice holds the Playbook's own tension on
purpose: **led by pride, delivering inclusion.** Under-the-lights game-night energy is the hook — being
an Eagle is aspirational, cool, something to belong to. The promise underneath is that
*nobody is turned away*. Confident, warm, direct; speaks to families and sponsors in plain,
kid-first language, never corporate. "Lead with the kids, not with winning."

Emotional target (public site, first five seconds): aspirational varsity pride that is
**unmistakably welcoming** — the game feels big *and* the door feels open.

## Anti-references

This must NOT look like:

- **A pity-driven charity page** — no sad-kid photography, no guilt-based "donate now." This is
  aspirational *access*, not charity for the unfortunate. Dignity over pity.
- **A generic rec-league portal** — no SignUpGenius/LeagueApps clip-art clutter, no
  sign-up-form-as-homepage aesthetic.
- **A corporate SaaS landing** — no purple gradient hero, no three identical feature cards, no
  tiny tracked-uppercase eyebrows above every section. (These are AI-template tells; see the
  skill's absolute bans.)
- **Loud youth-sports clip-art** — no flame graphics, angled neon, or trophy-and-star overload.

## Design Principles

1. **Grow our own, shown not told.** The pathway (Grassroot Eagles → Academy → Next XI →
   Varsity) is a real ladder — let the interface make the continuity visible and inevitable.
2. **Pride is the hook, "every kid" is the promise.** Every screen leads with aspiration and
   closes with an open door. If a family feels either sold-to or pitied, it's wrong.
3. **Make the ask small and clear.** One bounded, obvious next step per surface — sign up, or
   pick a sponsor tier. An open-ended dream is easy to defer; a specific ask is easy to grant.
4. **One system, both programs.** Boys and girls are equal and co-present everywhere — never
   an afterthought toggle, never boys-first.
5. **Phone-first, barrier-free.** Families decide on a phone; the site must be fast, legible,
   and bilingual-ready (Spanish where needed). Cost, jargon, and friction are the enemies.
6. **Additive, not instead.** The Academy is a free development-and-evaluation *overlay* on top
   of rec and travel — never another team to join. Frame it as working in the gaps (off-season,
   off-nights, weekend mornings, after-school) so a family never has to choose. What it adds that
   games don't: individual technique, small-sided creative play, and the Eagles system taught early.
7. **In the dashboard, red means a kid at risk of being lost** — the loudest signal in the
   system, not generic error chrome.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- **WCAG 2.1 AA** minimum. Body text ≥ 4.5:1; large/UI text ≥ 3:1. Verify contrast on the
  drenched-blue surfaces specifically — white-on-blue and blue-on-white both pass, muted
  gray-on-tint does not.
- **Phone-first & touch-friendly.** Design mobile up; tap targets ≥ 44px.
- **Bilingual-ready.** Copy structured so a Spanish version drops in (the Playbook sends flyers
  home in Spanish where needed). No idiom that won't translate.
- **Reduced motion is required.** Every animation needs a `prefers-reduced-motion` alternative.
- **Color-blind safe.** Never encode meaning in hue alone — pair the red "at-risk" signal with
  an icon/label; distinguish boys/girls by more than color.
